Bollywood Strike Ends Between Multiplex and Producers
Date: 06/05/2009:
A two month strike by the producers of Bollywood movies has now come to an end. The producers had gone no strike in April in regrads to the revenue sharing terms with the multiplex owners and not released any movie for the last two months. Now that the terms have been argreed to by both parties, Bollywood films would start to be on the big screen starting next Friday June 12. The first once out of the gates will be Vashu Bhagnani's Kal Kisne Dekha (2009) and Mithun Chakraborty starrer "Zor Laga Ke Haiya", followed by New York (2009) starring John Abraham and Katrina Kaif . The final settlement, 50, 42, 35 and 30 percent of collections in the first, second, third and fourth weeks respectively will be shared between the two parties.
TV Shows
05/14/2009:
On May 11, Nick India, started airing a new TV series based on Little Krishna. The 13 episode series, dubbed in Hindi and English, is India's first 3D animated TV series developed at the cost of 50 crore. It was developed by Reliance’s BIG Animation Studio in association with The Indian Heritage Foundation and was scripted by Jeffrey Scott, the man behind the Ice Age and Ninja Turtles television series. Parikshit Dasa of Lion King was in charge of art direction. The series created content that required 30 Terra Bytes of virtual space and was supported by gigabyte network through RX Foundry for smooth work flow process, while Blue Arc was used as the storage system.

India News
06/30/2009:
Sonia Gandhi , the head of the ruling Congress party, cut the ribbon to officially open the bridge to connect western suburbs of Bandra to Worli. The bridge took nine years of construction at cost of more then $400m. The bridge is over the Arabian Sea and has eight lanes and promised to reduce the travel time from 45 minutes to less then 10 minutes. The first of its kind in the country, the 5.6 km link was conceived in the 1990s. The two central towers that soar above the sea will change the city’s iconic skyline.

World News:
05/18/2009:
The high priced golden culinary herb saffron, made from crocus flowers, and used in cooking, may hold one of the keys to preventing the loss of sight in old age and could even help improve vision in people suffering from certain blinding eye diseases. A team found that saffron slows the loss of sight becuase the herb has remarkable effects on the genes which actually regulate the performance of the eye's key vision cells.
